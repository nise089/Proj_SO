The company’s life cycle

You will be randomly assigned to a company.
A company consists of one owner and three workers.
You will be randomly assigned one of the two roles.

The company‘s life cycle consists of (1) a founding phase and (2) a production phase.

In the founding phase, the owner receives an endowment of {{C.ENDOWMENT}} and can decide to keep it or to invest it as a loan to start the company.

  • If the money is kept, Part 2 ends. The owner receives the endowment, and the workers receive no payoff.
  • If the endowment is invested, Part 2 continues with the production phase. The investment is treated as a loan to the company. It will be repaid after the first round of the production phase.

The production phase has a maximum of {{C.ROUNDS}} rounds.
Each round of the production phase has three consecutive stages: a. working stage, b. profit choice stage, and c. selling stage.

  1. In the working stage, each worker performs a task, generating company profit.
  2. In the profit choice stage, the owner decides how the profit is spent.
  3. Finally, in the selling choice stage, the owner can decide whether to sell the company or not.
    • If the company is sold, the experiment ends, and each participant receives the payoff collected until this round.
    • If the company is not sold, the experiment continues with the next round until the last round or until the company is sold.
