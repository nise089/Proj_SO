The working stage

In this stage, the workers perform a slider task.
The task is to solve as many sliders as possible within {{C.SLIDERTIME}} minutes.
The total number of sliders solved determines the company’s revenue.
In detail, the company’s revenue is determined by the total number of sliders multiplied by the company’s productivity level of {{C.FIXED_REVENUE}} plus a fixed revenue of {{C.FIXED_REVENUE}} points:

𝑅𝑒𝑣𝑒𝑛𝑢𝑒 = {{C.PRODUCTIVITY}} * 𝑡𝑜𝑡𝑎𝑙 𝑛𝑢𝑚𝑏𝑒𝑟 𝑜𝑓 𝑠𝑙𝑖𝑑𝑒𝑟𝑠 + {{C.FIXED_REVENUE}}

The three workers receive a fixed wage of {{C.WAGE}} points for completing the task and a piece-rate wage of {{C.PIECERATE}} points for every correctly solved slider. In addition, the owner receives a fixed dividend of {{C.DIVIDEND}} points.

Thus, the company’s costs are
𝐶𝑜𝑠𝑡s= {{C.PIECERATE}}∗𝑡𝑜𝑡𝑎𝑙 𝑛𝑢𝑚𝑏𝑒𝑟 𝑜𝑓 𝑠𝑙𝑖𝑑𝑒𝑟𝑠 + {{C.DIVIDEND}}+{{C.N}}∗{{C.WAGE}} = {{cost_factor}}+{{C.PIECERATE}}*𝑡𝑜𝑡𝑎𝑙 𝑛𝑢𝑚𝑏𝑒𝑟 𝑜𝑓 𝑠𝑙𝑖𝑑𝑒𝑟𝑠

The company’s profit is determined by the revenue minus the costs.
P𝑟𝑜𝑓𝑖𝑡=({{C.PRODUCTIVITY}}-{{C.PIECERATE}})∗𝑡𝑜𝑡𝑎𝑙 𝑛𝑢𝑚𝑏𝑒𝑟 𝑜𝑓 𝑠𝑙𝑖𝑑𝑒𝑟𝑠 +({{C.FIXED_REVENUE}}−{{cost_factor}})= {{profit_factor}}*𝑡𝑜𝑡𝑎𝑙 𝑛𝑢𝑚𝑏𝑒𝑟 𝑜𝑓 𝑠𝑙𝑖𝑑𝑒𝑟𝑠 +{{profit_fixed}}

In short: The higher the number of solved sliders, the higher the profit.

Note that the profit can never be negative and is at least as high as the endowment, even if the workers solve zero sliders. Therefore, the endowment is always paid back.

In the first round, the owner’s loan is repaid such that there the profit is
